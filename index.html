WELCOME

This Advice in Havering Project aims to provide easy access to obtain free advice and information, mainly for people living or working within the London Borough of Havering. There are also some important national contacts.

There is a prime focus on Citizens Advice, who have been delivering a charitable service to the local Havering community for more than eighty years. They are one of the longest established organisations to supply free advice and support for people within the Borough.

Citizens Advice Havering is a registered charity and member of a national network with over 21,000 volunteers and 7,000 paid staff, providing a vital independent confidential advice and information service to the public in approximately 2,500 locations across England and Wales, completely free of charge.

In the last year more than 2.55 million peple were given individual advice, and the website advice pages had over 58 million views.

Citizens Advice in Romford

Citizens Advice in Romford, is the administration office for Citizens Advice Havering, and is conveniently situated close to the town centre, within the Romford Central Library building.

Address: 1st Floor, Romford Central Library, 1 Edwards Way, Romford. RM1 3AR

The Romford office does not provide a drop-in advice service. Limited face-to-face appointments can be booked at the Citizens Advice outreach offices around Havering, as well as at H.A.D. (Havering Association for People with Disabilities).

Citizens Advice in Romford is normally open to visitors on Mondays to Thursdays, between 10am and 12noon, for information and signposting only. There is no available ingoing public telephone number to the office.

Havering Information

Havering is the most easterly, and one of the largest Boroughs in London, covering a space of around 40 square miles, and is made up of about 50 percent of Green Belt Land.
Although mostly suburban, Havering does have a fair share of social and economic problems affecting many residents. The Council together with local voluntary charities and other publicly funded organisations, tries to provide services for issues that affect everyone living or working within the Borough.
